Working Business Model

Occam —
a working hypothesis.

Occam is an AI-native advisory and implementation firm. This page presents the current working model of how we create value, how we go to market, how we deliver, and how we diagnose. It is a living document for internal discussion and challenge.

The problem we solve

Growing businesses develop
operational complexity they can't outrun.

01 — Decision logic
Trapped in people

Key decisions depend on individuals who cannot scale. When those people aren't available, the business stalls. When they leave, the logic walks out with them.

02 — Manual workarounds
Processes held together by spreadsheets

Workflows that worked at smaller scale now require manual stitching. Data moves by email and copy-paste. Errors accumulate. Speed suffers.

03 — Fragmented systems
Tools that don't talk to each other

Systems were introduced at different stages to solve different problems. Nobody engineered them to connect. The integration gap becomes the operational bottleneck.

04 — Execution lag
The business moves slower than its strategy

Leadership sets direction but the operating model can't keep up. Decisions that should take hours take days. Opportunities are missed. Growth stalls at the operational ceiling.

Occam identifies the constraint and fixes it — faster than any conventional method.

Most organisations try to solve this with more software or more consulting. The deeper issue is that the underlying operating system of the business has never been properly engineered. We enter at the point of operational friction, translate messy business reality into structured, machine-readable logic, and use AI-assisted engineering to implement systems that remove the bottleneck.

The two offerings

Two ways to engage.
One coherent commercial logic.

Commercial logic: Pre-built packages create entry and diagnostic insight. Core advisory and implementation delivers the solution that removes the constraint — and therefore captures the majority of the value and revenue.

Offering 01
Pre-Built Packages

Structured, repeatable entry points that diagnose, frame, and accelerate engagement. Designed to reduce friction for new clients, create commercial clarity, and open the door to deeper work. They are not the whole business — they are the entry mechanism.

  • AI Readiness Score
  • AI Readiness Assessments
  • Operational opportunity diagnostic
  • Pillar-specific diagnostic scans
  • Rapid maturity assessments
Offering 02
Core Advisory & Implementation

The main engine of value creation. Bespoke, flexible, and high-value — delivered through the four-phase operating model and accelerated through AI-assisted engineering, which compresses timelines that traditionally took months into weeks. This is where the firm's real commercial weight sits and where fees should reflect the impact created.

  • Business analysis and operating model design
  • Solution architecture and technical specification
  • Rapid AI-assisted system development
  • System integration and workflow automation
  • Ongoing support, optimisation, and value tracking

Diagnosis framework

Six dimensions we assess,
design, and deliver across.

In packages, the six pillars diagnose. In consulting, they shape design and delivery. They are the intellectual architecture of Occam and the mechanism through which we determine where a business is blocked and what must change for the solution to work.

01
Strategy

Is AI genuinely connected to how the business competes — or running as a disconnected experiment?

02
Governance

Are compliance and risk engineered into operations, or bolted on as costly afterthoughts?

03
People

Can the workforce absorb AI without capability gaps, low adoption, or unmanaged displacement?

04 — Core
Business Engineering

Is the operating model structured in a way that AI can actually run inside it at scale? This is the central pillar.

05
Data

Is data sufficient, reliable, and accessible enough for real AI deployment — or still stuck in silos?

06
Value Realisation

Can the business define, measure, and financially attribute the value AI creates — with real discipline?

Discussion

Three questions this model
is asking you to challenge.

This is a working hypothesis. It should be stress-tested, not accepted. The three questions below are the most important unresolved tensions in the current model — and where honest challenge will create the most value.

  • Q1 Does this accurately describe what Occam does? Is the framing of “AI-native advisory and implementation firm” the right identity — or does it leave something important out, or overclaim something we have not yet earned? →
  • Q2 Are the two offerings correctly framed? Does the distinction between pre-built packages and core advisory and implementation hold up commercially — or is there a different structure that better reflects how clients actually engage with us? →
  • Q3 Are the six pillars the right diagnostic framework? Do they cover what actually matters in the engagements we run — or are some missing, some redundant, or some in the wrong sequence? →
