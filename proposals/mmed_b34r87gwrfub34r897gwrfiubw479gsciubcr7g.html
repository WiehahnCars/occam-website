The opportunity

The hidden margin problem in hospital operations.

01

Pathway variability

Hospitals rarely operate a single clinical pathway for a given outcome. In practice, multiple variations emerge across doctors, departments, ordering patterns, and billing practices — producing identical outcomes but materially different costs.

02

Fragmented systems

Operational and financial data typically sit in separate systems. As a result, variability in cost, billing, and reimbursement is rarely visible at the procedure or doctor level.

03

Where margin opportunity sits

Hospital margin opportunity typically sits in two areas.

In complex hospital systems, variability is often hidden within fragmented data and addressed qualitatively rather than measured for economic impact.

⚙
Revenue leakage

  • Coding & DRG optimisation
  • Reimbursement alignment
  • Denials & under-recovery
  • Case-mix classification accuracy

⚕
Clinical pathway variability

  • Length-of-stay variability
  • Drug substitution & generic utilisation
  • Pathology & radiology ordering patterns
  • Supply and consumable utilisation
Why this is hard to see

Financial impact is dispersed across hospital and billing systems and rarely visible at doctor or procedure level.

Clinical variation often appears justified, but its economic impact is rarely quantified.

Without integrated clinical and financial visibility, hospitals cannot systematically identify or act on these margin opportunities.
Initial hypothesis

Integrating data to surface hidden margin.

Integrating clinical activity data with billing and reimbursement data will reveal systematic variation in procedure pathways that materially impacts hospital profitability.

By making these variations visible at the level of procedures, doctors, and departments, Melomed can identify opportunities to:

  • reduce unnecessary cost
  • improve coding and reimbursement accuracy
  • standardise high-performing clinical pathways
  • without changing patient outcomes
By integrating these datasets and analysing procedure-level economics, hidden margin opportunities inside routine hospital activity can be systematically identified.
