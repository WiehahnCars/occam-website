The problem

Your operating model
is constraining cash velocity.

Your current processes have supported growth to this point — but fragmented systems, manual handoffs, and delayed proof-of-delivery are now locking up working capital and limiting your ability to invoice on time.

01 — Visibility
Fragmented execution visibility

No single end-to-end view from commercial intent through to physical delivery. Purchase Orders, Purchase Requisitions, and Collection Notes operate across disconnected layers.

02 — Documentation
Manual proof-of-delivery flow

POD documents are returned manually and asynchronously, delaying reconciliation and blocking timely invoicing after loads are physically completed.

03 — Working capital
Invoicing delayed up to 10 days

Invoicing is blocked until the full POD pack is received. Despite loads being physically complete, capital remains tied up for up to 10 days — an avoidable drag.

04 — Margin control
Commercial control gaps

Quoted margins are not systematically enforced at commitment. Execution realities (transport, split loads, route changes) mean margin outcomes are only understood after the fact.

running across fragmented systems.

Key change 01
PO-driven control

The customer PO becomes the primary commercial control point. Each PO creates one or more collection notes, and margin is validated at PO level and reconciled at load level.

Key change 02
Same-day POD capture

Proof of Delivery is captured at the point of delivery via a lightweight driver web app or WhatsApp. This automatically triggers the invoicing workflow in Sage — eliminating the 10-day delay.

Key change 03
End-to-end visibility

The Intechem Command Layer connects commercial intent (quotes, POs) to physical execution (collection notes, loads) to cash (invoicing) — through a single, integrated layer.

Why not a PoC?

A PoC is not commercially
realistic for this problem.

Building a production system immediately

  • ✕Key assumptions remain unvalidated, risking rework mid-build
  • ✕Scope changes and cost escalation become likely outcomes
  • ✕Operational disruption if the system doesn't match actual workflows
  • ✕A "low-cost PoC" would be either overly simplified or insufficient to validate the real workflow
  • ✕Technical effort (integration, security, workflow logic) cannot be meaningfully reduced

Design sprint first — the preferred path

  • ✓Lock down requirements precisely before committing to build
  • ✓Validate technical feasibility with your actual systems (Sage, Dynamics)
  • ✓Confirm integration complexity so there are no surprises at implementation
  • ✓Price the implementation accurately — one quote, no change orders
  • ✓Intechem retains the blueprint regardless of who builds it
